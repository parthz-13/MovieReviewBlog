MOVIE OVERVIEW

SYNOPSIS

"Exhuma" is a South Korean supernatural horror film released in 2024, directed by Jang Jae-hyun. The movie follows a group of shamans and a feng shui master who are hired by a wealthy Korean-American family to investigate a mysterious family curse. When they discover a dark and disturbing secret buried in the family's ancestral grave, they become entangled in a terrifying supernatural event that threatens to unleash an ancient and malevolent force.

CAST

• Choi Min-sik (playing a feng shui master)
• Kim Go-eun (playing a shaman)
• Lee Do-hyun
• Yoo Hae-jin

REVIEW

"Exhuma" has been widely praised for its unique blend of traditional Korean spiritual beliefs, horror elements, and psychological tension. Critics have commended the film for its atmospheric storytelling and the nuanced performances of its lead actors, particularly Choi Min-sik and Kim Go-eun. The movie stands out in the horror genre by deeply incorporating Korean cultural and spiritual practices into its narrative.

The film expertly weaves together elements of supernatural horror with cultural commentary, exploring themes of family history, ancestral sins, and the lingering power of past traumas. Its slow-burn approach to horror, combined with stunning cinematography and a haunting soundtrack, creates a deeply unsettling viewing experience.
