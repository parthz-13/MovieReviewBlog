MOVIE OVERVIEW

SYNOPSIS

Directed by Yeon Sang-ho, Train to Busan is a gripping South Korean action-horror film that redefines the zombie genre. The story follows Seok-woo, a workaholic fund manager, who boards a train from Seoul to Busan with his estranged daughter, Su-an, to reunite her with her mother. As a zombie outbreak ravages South Korea, the passengers on the train must fight for survival against hordes of the undead. The journey becomes a high-stakes battle for survival and humanity, as alliances form, sacrifices are made, and the thin line between selfishness and heroism is tested.

REVIEW

Train to Busan combines relentless action with heartfelt emotion, delivering a zombie thriller that transcends the genre. Yeon Sang-ho’s direction is taut and dynamic, maintaining an intense pace while allowing moments of quiet reflection on human connections and morality.

The film excels in its character-driven narrative, with Gong Yoo giving a standout performance as Seok-woo, whose personal growth anchors the story. Kim Su-an shines as Su-an, delivering a touching portrayal of innocence amidst chaos. Ma Dong-seok steals the show with his commanding yet endearing presence as Sang-hwa, blending humor, strength, and vulnerability.

The confined train setting amplifies the tension, with claustrophobic sequences and innovative use of the environment making the action scenes unforgettable. The makeup and effects for the zombies are top-notch, creating genuinely terrifying adversaries. Beyond the horror and thrills, the film explores themes of sacrifice, redemption, and societal critique, leaving a lasting emotional impact.
